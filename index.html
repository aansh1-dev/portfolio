Case Study Analysis

Nike Strategy
Brand Analysis

The Objective

"I wanted to see how Nike pulls in so many people and keeps them buying, especially when a lot of it is just because of the brand name and reputation."

01 Emotional Branding

Nike pushes motivation and confidence. But as a giant, many buy it now simply because they trust the name.

02 Storytelling

Showing real athlete struggles and wins. Many buy the swoosh now because it feels premium.

03 Consistency

Keeping everything clean and visuals the same builds brand value so high that people buy automatically.

04 Trust & Status

Teaming with pros creates an aspirational "cool." Social proof drives comfort.

Strategy Radar

Key Learnings

Market Trust

Trust in name and quality sustains scale once established.

Sales Velocity

A strong name sells products automatically without story.

Loyalty Loop

Consistency builds value where people don't think twice.

Social Proof

Collabs add status; everyone having it adds comfort.

Primary UX Research | April 2026

Swiggy vs Zomato
UX Case Study

The Objective

I placed the same order (Domino's Big Big 6in1 Cheeseburst, ₹999) on both Swiggy and Zomato and screenshotted every step of the flow. Here's what I found:

1 / 4 • Audit Evidence

01 Price Transparency

• Both apps show ₹999 on the restaurant page

• Swiggy checkout: ₹1,111 final (₹111.63 added as "GST & Other Charges" — no breakdown)

• Zomato checkout: ₹1,113.63 final (itemised: ₹42 packaging + ₹14.90 fee + ₹54.73 GST + ₹3 donation)

• Result: Swiggy inflates cart price by 11.2% at checkout with zero explanation. Zomato is slightly more expensive but fully transparent.

02 Coupon UX

• Swiggy coupon page opens with "Items added to your cart are not eligible for any coupons" — then shows 3 coupons anyway, all requiring more spend

• Zomato auto-applied ₹1,150 discount before checkout without user doing anything

• Zomato coupon page shows exact savings per code (₹33.41, ₹55.68) calculated for your current cart

03 Navigation

• Swiggy: 5 service tabs + banner carousel + promotional tiles before restaurant results — promotion-first design

• Zomato: Category chips, filter buttons, "Healthy Mode" tab — more user-controlled navigation

• Both apps show competitor ads in search — Zomato labels them clearly as "Ad"

04 Verdict

"Zomato wins on transparency and coupon UX. Swiggy wins on service breadth. On pure food ordering experience, Zomato is more honest at the moments that matter most."

Comparison Radar

Recommendations

Rec 1 (Swiggy)

Only show applicable coupons at checkout.

Rec 2 (Swiggy)

Break down "GST & Other Charges" into itemized line items.

Rec 3 (Zomato)

Reduce competitor ad density in direct restaurant searches.

Rec 4 (Both)

Show estimated final price (incl. charges) on the restaurant page, before checkout.
